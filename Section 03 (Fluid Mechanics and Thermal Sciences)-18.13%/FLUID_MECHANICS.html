\(\Rightarrow\) Dimensionless Numbers
(i) Prandtl number (Pr) The Prandtl number is a dimensionless parameter used in fluid mechanics and heat transfer to characterize the relative importance of momentum diffusion to thermal diffusion. \( Pr = \frac{\nu}{\alpha} \)
(ii) Grashof number (Gr) The Grashof number is a dimensionless parameter that compares buoyancy forces to viscous forces in fluid flows, particularly in natural convection. Gr = \(\frac{g\beta \Delta T L^3}{\nu^2}\)
(iii) Sherwood number (ShL) The Sherwood number represents the ratio of convective mass transfer rate to mass transfer by molecular diffusion. ShL = \(\frac{h_mL}{D}\)
(iv) Schmidt number (Sc) The Schmidt number represents the ratio of molecular diffusivity of momentum to molecular diffusivity of mass transfer. Sc = \(\frac{\nu}{D}\) or Sc = \(\frac{\mu}{\rho D}\)
(v) Rayleigh number (Ra) The Rayleigh number is used to predict the onset of natural convection in a fluid and is also used to calculate the heat transfer coefficient in natural convection. \(Ra = Gr. Pr = \frac{g\beta \Delta TL_c^3}{\nu^2} . \frac{\nu}{\alpha}\) \(\Rightarrow Ra = \frac{g\beta \Delta T L^3}{\nu \alpha}\)
\(\Rightarrow\) Darcy's friction factor This expression approximates the friction factor (\(f\)) in laminar flow (\(Re < 2000\)). The value of 64 is derived from the Hagen-Poiseuille equation for smooth circular pipes. It is a simplified model for fully developed laminar flow. \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\) (Emperical)
This equation relates the friction factor (\(f\)) to the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) in the transitional flow regime (2000 < \(Re\) < 4000). It provides an approximation for the friction factor in cases where laminar and turbulent flow characteristics coexist. \(f = \frac{0.316}{Re^{1/4}}\) (Emperical)
\(\Rightarrow\) Derivation of Bernoulli equation (Video) The Bernoulli equation states that \(\frac{P}{\rho g} + \frac{v^2}{2g} + Z = \text{Const.}\)
\(\Rightarrow\) Pressure coefficient (\(C_p\)) The pressure coefficient measures the difference in static pressure at a point compared to the free stream static pressure. It is given by \(C_p = \frac{P - P_{\text{free}}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho V^2}\)
\(\Rightarrow\) Darcy-Weisbach Equation The Darcy-Weisbach equation is an empirical formula used to calculate the head loss due to friction in a pipe or conduit. \(h_f = \frac{{fLv^2}}{{2gd}}\quad\).....(Empirical)
\(\Rightarrow\) Reynolds Number The Reynolds number is a dimensionless quantity used to characterize the flow regime of a fluid. It represents the ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces and is used to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. \(\text{{Re}} = \frac{{\text{{Inertia Force}}}}{{\text{{Viscous Force}}}}\)
\(\Rightarrow\) Boundary Layer Theory
Laminar Boundary Layer (Video) For laminar boundary layers, the velocity profile can be described by a parabolic equation in terms of the distance \(y\) from the surface and the boundary layer thickness (\(\delta\)). \(\frac{U}{U_{\infty}} = 2(\frac{y}{\delta})-(\frac{y}{\delta})^2\)
Turbulent Boundary Layer (Video) For turbulent boundary layers, the velocity profile can be described by a power-law equation in terms of \(y\) and \(\delta\). \(\frac{U}{U_{\infty}} = \frac{y}{\delta}^{1/7}\)
Displacement Thickness (Video) The displacement thickness (\(\delta^*\)) is a measure of how much the boundary layer displaces the outer flow due to the presence of the body (Conservation of mass). \(\delta^* = \int_{0}^{\delta} (1-\frac{U}{U_{\infty}})dy\)
Momentum Thickness (Video) The momentum thickness (\(\theta\)) represents the distance by which the boundary layer would need to be displaced outward to maintain the same mass flux as the actual boundary layer. \(\theta = \int_{0}^{\delta} \frac{U}{U_{\infty}}(1-\frac{U}{U_{\infty}})dy\)
Energy Thickness (Video) The energy thickness (\(\delta_e\)) is a measure of the distance by which the boundary layer would need to be displaced outward to maintain the same energy flux as the actual boundary layer. \(\delta_e = \int_{0}^{\delta} \frac{U}{U_{\infty}}(1-\frac{U^2}{U_{\infty}^2})dy\)
Local Skin Friction Drag Coefficient (Video) \(C_{fx} = \frac{\tau_{wx}}{\frac{1}{2}\rho U_{\infty}^2}\)
Average Skin Friction Drag Coefficient \(C_{D} = \frac{F_D}{\frac{1}{2}\rho U_{\infty}^2A}\)
