\(\Rightarrow\) Hydraulic machines
(i) Power of turbine/pump The power of a turbine or pump can be calculated using the formula \(Power = \eta \rho gQH\)
(ii) The formula for specific speed The specific speed (\(N_s\)) is given by \(N_s = \frac{N \sqrt{Q}}{H^{3/4}}\)
(iii) Non-dimensional specific speed The non-dimensional specific speed (\(N_s\)) is given by \(N_s = \frac{N \sqrt{P}}{(gH)^{5/4} \sqrt{\rho}}\)
Range for non-dimensional specific speed Less than 0.18: Single jet Pelton
0.18 - 0.30: Multi Jet Pelton
0.30 - 1.50: Francis
1.50 - 5.12: Kaplan
(iv) a) Pelton Turbine Working Principle: The Pelton turbine is an impulse turbine designed for high-head applications. It uses high-velocity jets of water to strike the turbine wheel's buckets and generate rotational motion.
b) Francis Turbine Working Principle: The Francis turbine is a reaction turbine that operates on both impulse and reaction forces. Water enters the turbine through a spiral casing and flows over the blades of the runner, generating rotational motion.
c) Kaplan Turbine Working Principle: The Kaplan turbine is a propeller-type reaction turbine designed for low-head applications. It features adjustable blades to optimize performance across a range of flow rates and heads.
